A BRIEF HISTORY OF USA HOCKEY

The US National Ice Hockey Teams Maintain Competitiveness - Sometimes Dominance

With over 100 years of history on the national stage, US national teams have been largely successful; albeit with some noticable disappointments.

The U.S. Men's Olympic Team has won two gold medals, eight silver medals and one bronze medal. The most recent medal for the U.S. was at the 2010 Olympic Games in Vancouver, when they took home silver. The men's team has competed in 22 Olympic Games starting in 1920 and most recently in 2022.

At the most recent Winter Olympic Games (Beijing in 2022), the U.S. Men's team finished in fifth place.

Looking forward to the next Winter Olympic Games, being held in Italy in 2026, the U.S. men's team hopes to qualify and pursue their third Gold medal.

Women's ice hockey has only been included in the Olympic Games since 1998. Since then, the U.S. Women's national team has qualified and earned a medal in every single Olympic Games tournament.

The U.S. women's team took home gold at the inaugural tournament in Nagano, Japan in a thrilling gold-medal game over the rival Canadians. In total, the U.S. has won two gold medals (1998 & 2018), four silver medals (2002, 2010, 2014, 2022) and one bronze medal (2006).

The U.S. Women's team has very high expectations for the upcoming 2026 Winter Olympics - being held in Italy.
